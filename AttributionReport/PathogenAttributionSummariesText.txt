Among the pathogens and food categories considered, eggs were associated with the greatest cost of foodborne illness, with a total cost of 134 million AUD. Of this total, 51.3 million AUD was due to Non-typhoidal Salmonella, 45.8 million AUD was due to Norovirus, 28.5 million AUD was due to Listeria monocytogenes, and 4.23 million AUD was due to STEC. The food category associated with the most cases of foodborne illness circa 2019 was also eggs, with a total burden of 142,000 cases. Of this total, 116,000 cases were due to Norovirus, 22,300 cases were due to Non-typhoidal Salmonella, 952 cases were due to STEC, and 36 cases were due to Listeria monocytogenes.

Listeria monocytogenes resulted in an annual cost of approximately 78.4 million AUD circa 2019 arising from 100 cases of illness, 100 hospitalisations, and 15 deaths. Eggs were the leading source (36%) with a total annual cost of 28.5 million AUD arising from 36 cases of illness, 36 hospitalisations, and 6 deaths. The next three most frequent sources were poultry (24%), pork (9%), and vegetables (8%).

Non-typhoidal Salmonella resulted in an annual cost of approximately 140 million AUD circa 2019 arising from 61,100 cases of initial illness, 11,100 cases of sequel illness (reactive arthritis or irritable bowel syndrome), 4,470 hospitalisations, and 12 deaths. Eggs were the leading source (37%) with a total annual cost of 51.3 million AUD arising from 22,300 cases of initial illness, 4,060 cases of sequel illness, 1,630 hospitalisations, and 4 deaths. The next three most frequent sources were poultry (24%), pork (9%), and vegetables (8%).

Norovirus resulted in an annual cost of approximately 129 million AUD circa 2019 arising from 328,000 cases of illness, 1,530 hospitalisations, and 1 death. Eggs were the leading source (36%) with a total annual cost of 45.8 million AUD arising from 116,000 cases of illness, 553 hospitalisations, and 0 deaths. The next three most frequent sources were poultry (23%), pork (8%), and vegetables (8%).

STEC resulted in an annual cost of approximately 11.6 million AUD circa 2019 arising from 2,640 cases of initial illness, 78 cases of sequel illness (haemolytic uremic syndrome), 112 hospitalisations, and 2 deaths. Eggs were the leading source (36%) with a total annual cost of 4.23 million AUD arising from 952 cases of initial illness, 28 cases of sequel illness, 41 hospitalisations, and 1 deaths. The next three most frequent sources were poultry (24%), pork (9%), and vegetables (8%).
