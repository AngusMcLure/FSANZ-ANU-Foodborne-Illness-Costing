Among the pathogens and food categories considered, chicken was associated with the greatest cost of foodborne illness, with a total cost of 66.6 million AUD. Of this total 55.2 million AUD was due to Non-typhoidal Salmonella, 5.49 million AUD was due to Norovirus, 3.83 million AUD was due to Listeria monocytogenes, and 0.559 million AUD was due to STEC. The food category associated with the most cases of foodborne illness circa 2019 was also chicken, with a total burden of 40,800 cases. Of this total, 23,200 cases were due to Non-typhoidal Salmonella, 13,700 cases were due to Norovirus, 119 cases were due to STEC, and 5 cases were due to Listeria monocytogenes.

Non-typhoidal Salmonella resulted in an annual cost of approximately 140 million AUD circa 2019 arising from 61,500 cases of initial illness, 11,100 cases of sequel illness (reactive arthritis or irritable bowel syndrome), 4,460 hospitalisations, and 12 deaths. Chicken was the leading source (39%) with a total annual cost of 55.2 million AUD arising from 23,200 cases of initial illness, 3,970 cases of sequel illness, 1,760 hospitalisations, and 4 deaths. The next three most frequent sources were eggs (39%), beef (5%), and pork (5%).

Norovirus resulted in an annual cost of approximately 128 million AUD circa 2019 arising from 326,000 cases of illness, 1,530 hospitalisations, and 1 death. Beef was the leading source (4%) with a total annual cost of 5.5 million AUD arising from 13,700 cases of illness, 71 hospitalisations, and 0 deaths. The next three most frequent sources were eggs (4%), chicken (4%), and pork (4%).

Listeria monocytogenes resulted in an annual cost of approximately 78.4 million AUD circa 2019 arising from 101 cases of illness, 101 hospitalisations, and 15 deaths. Dairy was the leading source (49%) with a total annual cost of 38.2 million AUD arising from 45 cases of illness, 45 hospitalisations, and 7 deaths. The next three most frequent sources were beef (5%), pork (5%), and chicken (5%).

STEC resulted in an annual cost of approximately 11.7 million AUD circa 2019 arising from 2,620 cases of initial illness, 78 cases of sequel illness (haemolytic uremic syndrome), 112 hospitalisations, and 2 deaths. Chicken was the leading source (5%) with a total annual cost of 0.559 million AUD arising from 119 cases of initial illness, 3 cases of sequel illness, 5 hospitalisations, and 0 deaths. The next three most frequent sources were pork (5%), beef (5%), and eggs (5%).
